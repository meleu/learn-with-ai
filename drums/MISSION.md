# Mission

## The one-sentence mission

Play **faster, cleaner single strokes** — raise the tempo at which I can play
even, controlled single strokes (16th notes) with a metronome, without tension
or breakdown.

## Who I am

- Intermediate drummer.
- Disciplined and patient — will use practice time deliberately, not just bash.
- Always practice with a metronome.

## Constraints

- **30 minutes per day.** This is the hard budget every plan must fit inside.

## Why this matters

Single-stroke speed is a foundational limiter. Faster, cleaner singles unlock
fills, faster grooves, rolls, and general fluency around the kit. It is the
clearest, most measurable "chops" goal an intermediate player can chase.

## What success looks like

- A repeatable daily 30-minute routine driven by the metronome.
- A rising, **honest** "clean ceiling" BPM for continuous 16th-note singles.
- Speed that holds up *relaxed* — no clenching, no forearm burn, no flamming
  between the hands.

## Open questions (to refine the plan)

- Current clean-ceiling tempo (measured in Lesson 1).
- Grip: matched or traditional?
- Practice surface: practice pad, drum/kit, or both?
- Target tempo and rough timeframe.
